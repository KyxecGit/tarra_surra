The Sura Atmosphere

Beverage Director

Robert Shamblin

Since arriving in Chicago in 2015, Robert Shamblin has built a reputation as one of the city's most innovative and thoughtful beverage professionals. He has developed beverage programs recognized by Forbes, Time Out, Eater, and the Chicago Tribune.

Robert's approach to cocktails is rooted in four guiding principles: Context — every drink must reflect the setting and occasion; Respond or Reflect — each creation either mirrors or thoughtfully contrasts the food; International Connection — drawing on global flavors and techniques; and Memory Association — crafting drinks that evoke places, moments, and emotions.

At Sura, Robert brings this philosophy to life — building a bar program that is as intentional and layered as the dining experience at Tarra, while celebrating the vibrant cocktail culture of Bangkok.

"Cocktails provide a sense of context — they tell a story about where you are and what you're about to experience." — Robert Shamblin
Approach

Bar Philosophy

01

Context

Every drink begins with the setting. Robert designs cocktails that feel inseparable from the moment — whether it's an intimate dinner pairing or a late-night conversation at the bar.

02

Respond or Reflect

A great cocktail either mirrors the flavors on the plate or offers a deliberate contrast. Each drink is crafted in dialogue with Chef Namo's menu.

03

International Connection

Drawing on global spirits, techniques, and traditions — from Japanese whisky to Latin American agave — Robert creates drinks that transcend borders while honoring their origins.

04

Memory Association

The best drinks evoke something personal — a place, a season, a feeling. Robert's cocktails are designed to create lasting sensory memories.
