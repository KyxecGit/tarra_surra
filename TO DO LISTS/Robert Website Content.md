# Short Introduction

Beverage Director Robert Shamblin moved to Chicago in early 2015 with a thirst for culture and community. In short time he was convinced that the city’s expansive culinary scene served as the nexus of both, and left the 9-to-5 workforce to start a new career in hospitality.

Over the last decade he has crafted beverage programs in both award winning fine dining restaurants and neighborhood watering holes, garnering recognition in publications including Forbes, Time Out, and Eater.

Tarra presents a new adventure. With a broad palette of flavors from the Thai culinary tradition, Shamblin’s intention is to contextualize and frame the menu to emphasize key components of Chef Namo’s take on authentic Thai cuisine.

# Bio

Beverage Director Robert Shamblin has developed more than 30 seasonal cocktail menus for seven restaurants, opening several as a beverage consultant.

Shamblin began in marketing, but ditched the suit and tie to pursue a career in hospitality. At 26 he took a job clearing tables at A10 Hyde Park. Five months later, he was bartending six nights a week, learning everything he could from Charlie Trotters’ alumnus Chef Matthias Merges and Beverage Director Alex Bachman. After three seasons he took a position on the opening team at Bellemore to learn alongside former Billy Sunday Beverage Director Lee Zaremba. Soon after, he accepted the role of lead bartender at The Promontory, which received a Jean Banchet nomination for Best Neighborhood Bar.

In 2018, Shamblin joined BLVD as lead bartender and mixologist. His seasonal programs drew

recognition in several trade publications including Forbes, Eater, Time Out, Imbibe, Chilled, and

Dineit4ward. In 2020, with indoor dining restricted, Shamblin took a line cook position at L&M Fine Foods in Lincoln Square, sharpening his knife skills and leading him to a more culinary approach to menu creation. From there, he began a career as a beverage consultant, providing creative services for new restaurants and neighborhood staples.

Now working alongside Chef Namo, Shamblin has developed an authentic Thai cocktail bar in the heart of Chicago. At Tarra, cocktails feature uniquely Thai ingredients, but maintain a connection to the classics. Shamblin insists that cocktail culture is inherently international. A guest may not know the taste of betel leaf, but once tasting betel leaf infused gin in a white negroni, may come to love its nuanced flavor. Likewise, the wine list receives careful consideration, with selections that respond to the dishes, relaxing and refreshing the palate and creating harmony with the bold, aromatic ingredients in each course.

Sura has a different nature altogether. While Tarra will feature familiar drinks imbued with

traditional Thai ingredients, at Sura, Shamblin showcases these flavors in conceptually innovative and delicately balanced cocktails. Juices are transformed into stable air, punches clarify for days, and ferments evolve over weeks; Shamblin’s meticulous preparation ensures guests experience an enchanting culinary adventure that feels effortless.
