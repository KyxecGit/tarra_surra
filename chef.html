The Visionary

Chef Namo

Two Michelin stars. Fifteen years of mastery. A lifetime devoted to elevating the soul of Thai cuisine.

Biography

A Story of Passion

Born in the highlands of Chiang Mai, Chef Namo grew up surrounded by the intoxicating aromas of his grandmother's kitchen — fragrant curries simmering over charcoal, hand-pounded pastes bursting with lemongrass and galangal.

At just seventeen, he left Thailand to study classical French technique at Le Cordon Bleu in Paris, where he graduated top of his class. But it was the longing for home — for the bold, unapologetic flavors of Thai cooking — that pulled him back.

After apprenticeships at Gaggan (Bangkok), Nahm (Bangkok), and The Fat Duck (UK), Chef Namo opened Tarra in 2011 with a singular vision: to create a dining experience that honors Thai traditions while pushing every boundary of modern gastronomy.

Today, Tarra holds two Michelin stars and a place among Asia's 50 Best Restaurants. But for Chef Namo, the greatest reward is a guest's first bite — that moment when flavor becomes feeling.

"I don't cook Thai food. I cook feelings — the warmth of a grandmother's embrace, the chaos of a Bangkok street, the silence of a temple at dawn." — Chef Namo
Approach

Philosophy

01

Respect the Roots

Every dish begins with traditional Thai recipes — passed down through generations, unchanged in their essence. We honor the farmers, the fishermen, the grandmothers who created these flavors.

02

Elevate with Technique

Classical French precision meets Thai intuition. We use modern techniques not to replace tradition, but to reveal new dimensions within it — a deeper caramelization, a more delicate texture.

03

Source with Purpose

We work directly with over 30 Thai farms and fisheries. Every ingredient is chosen at its peak — from organic lemongrass in Chiang Rai to wild-caught prawns from the Andaman Sea.

04

Create an Experience

A meal at Tarra is more than food. It is theatre, emotion, and memory — designed to engage all five senses and leave a lasting impression on the soul.

Journey

Career Milestones

1994

Early Beginnings

Begins cooking alongside his grandmother in Chiang Mai, learning the ancient art of Thai curry pastes and fermented flavors.

2002

Le Cordon Bleu, Paris

Graduates top of his class in culinary arts, specializing in French patisserie and sauce technique.

2005

The Fat Duck, UK

Apprenticeship under Heston Blumenthal, exploring molecular gastronomy and multi-sensory dining.

2007

Gaggan, Bangkok

Sous chef at Asia's legendary progressive Indian restaurant, honing boundary-pushing creative vision.

2011

Tarra Opens

Opens Tarra on Sukhumvit Road — a modern Thai fine dining restaurant that quickly captivates Bangkok's culinary scene.

2015

First Michelin Star

Tarra receives its first Michelin star, praised for "extraordinary Thai cuisine elevated with impeccable technique."

2019

Second Michelin Star

The coveted second star arrives. Tarra enters Asia's 50 Best Restaurants at position #12.

2023

Sura Bar Launches

Chef Namo opens Sura — an adjoining cocktail bar that brings the same creative philosophy to mixology.
